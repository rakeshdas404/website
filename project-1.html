Workforce Salary Analysis

Analyzing Workforce Salary Trends and Variability Across Industries During the Covid-19 Period

View Report

Project Overview

The Covid-19 pandemic has dramatically reshaped the global workforce, impacting industries and job markets in ways that vary widely across sectors. This analysis focuses on understanding these shifts by examining salary trends, variability, and geographical differences within the workforce, using a dataset from the “Ask a Manager” survey. This dataset, drawn from self-reported information across various industries, offers insights into how salaries have evolved since the onset of the pandemic and sheds light on patterns that are both industry-specific and region-specific. The dataset provided contains several "free text" fields, reflecting the varied, often messy input from survey respondents. Cleaning and organizing this data is a vital first step to accurately interpret salary trends. In this project, we explore questions such as which industries report the highest and lowest salaries, highlighting sectors that appear to be thriving versus those facing more challenges. We further investigate the degree of salary variability within industries to identify fields with the widest income disparities, providing insights into the levels of stability and opportunity present across sectors.

Beyond industry comparisons, this analysis examines salary patterns over time and geography. Understanding how salaries shift by region and evolve year-over-year offers valuable context for assessing the ongoing effects of the pandemic on the workforce. This multifaceted approach provides a comprehensive view of the economic landscape, helping identify key trends, potential areas of growth, and sectors most in need of support as the workforce continues to adapt to new realities.
